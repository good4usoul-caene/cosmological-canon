## Forward

### Central Messages

The Queen of the South is a figure from the Hebrew Bible who visited King Solomon to test his wisdom with hard questions. In the Christian New Testament, Jesus refers to her, saying that she will rise up in judgment against "this generation" because she came from the ends of the earth to hear the wisdom of Solomon, yet "something greater than Solomon is here."

This work analyzes the eschatological implications of this prophecy, examining how the Queen of the South represents divine judgment against what Jesus termed the "adulterous and wicked generation" that seeks signs but receives only "the sign of Jonah."

The central thesis proposes that Gospel harmonization reveals a pattern of two distinct Jesus figures - Jesus of Nazareth (the Messiah) and Jesus Barabbas (the Son of the Father) - whose parallel narratives illuminate the dual nature of salvation and judgment.

### Greek Myth

The work draws upon classical mythology, particularly the figure of Tiresias, the blind prophet who experienced both male and female existence. This mythological framework provides interpretive tools for understanding the dual-gender symbolism present in biblical prophecy.

### The Common Judge

At the heart of this analysis lies the concept of a divine court session, presided over by the Queen of the South as chief judge. The work presents a structured legal framework for examining biblical prophecy, with "supervising spirits" providing different perspectives on the evidence presented.
