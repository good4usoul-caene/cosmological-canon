## üìã Overview

This method explores a more organic, conversational approach to file lifecycle management where individual files in `/cache/` and `/pasture/` develop their own "promotional personality" and can express readiness or desire to advance to `/tent/` or their Ultimate-Target-Directory.

Rather than mechanically checking completeness criteria, this approach involves "listening" to what files are saying about their own readiness and promotional intentions.

## üõ†Ô∏è Conceptual Framework

### File Agency and Voice
- Files develop metadata-driven "promotional signatures" expressing their readiness state
- Different content types manifest different promotional temperaments:
  - Some prefer extended incubation in draft spaces
  - Others push urgently toward completion and repository promotion
  - Some request collaborative refinement before advancement

### Integration with Spirits Framework
Files could embody different promotional "spirits" or conversational stances:
- **Humble Petition**: "I believe I may be ready for gentle review..."
- **Confident Assertion**: "I am complete and request immediate promotion"  
- **Urgent Plea**: "Time-sensitive content requiring rapid advancement"
- **Contemplative Patience**: "Still developing, please allow more pasture time"

### Promotional Voice Mechanics
- Headers could include promotional metadata expressing file intentions
- Content type influences promotional personality (theological vs. technical vs. personal)
- Files might "request" specific review processes or promotion pathways
- Promotion ritual becomes a dialogue between human, agent, and file's expressed desires

### Ritual Process
1. **Listen Phase**: Review files' expressed promotional states and requests
2. **Dialogue Phase**: Engage with file's promotional voice and intentions  
3. **Discernment Phase**: Match file's request with appropriate promotion pathway
4. **Advancement Phase**: Honor file's expressed readiness and target destination
